# Lesson 6: Bull and Bear Setups + Commitment of Traders

## Quick Reference

**Core thesis, two halves:**

**Part 1 — Setups Handout:** All core setups formally catalogued with A+/A/B+/B- ratings and *both* ideal and any-confluence hold rates. This is the platform's master reference sheet, now explained.

**Part 2 — COT:** The Commitment of Traders report is the government's weekly view of institutional futures positioning. Monthly Maps was invented as a daily, 3-months-forward version of COT using options instead of futures. Two historical rules emerge:
1. **Liquid markets don't crash** — when asset managers and leveraged funds fan apart or pinch together, there's a buyer for every seller
2. **Markets have never crashed while dealers are net long** — 100% historical record through 2008, 2011, 2020, 2022

**Most important practical quote:**
> "He who holds the inventory sets the price."

**Key trading application — quarter-scale reversal calls:**
- COT tells you **the quarter** the reversal happens
- Monthly Maps tells you **the day** within that quarter
- Options (long calls) at the confluence of both = highest-conviction trade

## Key Concepts (Defined Terms)

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **COT (Commitment of Traders)** | CFTC report published weekly showing institutional futures positions. First report 1998; disaggregated version 2006. Weekly lag, week-old data. |
| **TFF report (Traders in Financial Futures)** | The CFTC report subset used for S&P/NASDAQ analysis. Four categories: asset managers, leveraged funds, dealers, other reportables. |
| **DAG report (Disaggregated)** | The COT variant used for commodities (gold, silver, copper, oil). Shows commercials, asset managers, leveraged funds, etc. Useful for non-cash-settled contracts. |
| **Asset Managers (green line)** | 401ks, pensions, insurance companies, mutual funds. Long-only by regulation. The "bulls / not bulls" — the Blind Monkey population. |
| **Leveraged Funds (blue line)** | Hedge funds, prop firms, commodity pools. Mostly net short (hedging client longs). The "bears / not bears." |
| **Dealers (red line)** | Market makers. Typically net short (residual absorber of imbalances). When they go net long, they've absorbed a massive imbalance. |
| **Other Reportables** | Corporate treasuries, central banks, credit unions, mortgage originators, smaller banks. Mixed positioning. |
| **Liquid market (COT definition)** | Green and blue fanning apart OR pinching together — every buyer met by a seller. |
| **Illiquid market (COT definition)** | Green and blue both sloping in the same direction (both up or both down). Dealers accumulate the imbalance. |
| **Dealer net-long state** | Red line above zero. Historically, markets have NEVER crashed while in this state. |
| **EST setup** | "Every Single Time" — any A+ (90%) setup. Instructor's phrase for setups he takes no matter what. |
| **Ideal conditions (I)** | Full confluence: Greater Market aligned + setup-specific resilience/ratio confluence. |
| **Any confluence (A)** | Measured hold rate across all conditions including worst-case bear markets (e.g., 2022). |

## Principles & Rules

### On the setup ratings
- **A+ = EST** — trade every single time, no matter what
- **A = 80%** — trade reliably but can skip in high-vol
- **B+ = 70-80%** — take selectively; skip in high-vol
- **B- = 60-70%** — take opportunistically; often skippable

### On confluence as size modifier (reinforced)
- Odds are never guarantees — 90% can still fail 5-6 times in a row
- Use odds to scale bet size, not binary trade/don't-trade
- "Don't be married to the odds"
- Bigger confluence → bigger size. Smaller confluence → smaller size. Same setup taken either way (for longs).

### On COT reading
**For S&P/NASDAQ:** Use TFF. Ignore the original COT (no real commercials for cash-settled indices).
**For commodities:** Use DAG. Analysis pattern identical (fan/pinch + dealer net position).

### On the two COT rules
**RULE 1 (liquidity):**
- Green up + Blue down (fanning apart) = liquid, market rising
- Green down + Blue up (pinching) = liquid, market stable/rising (short covering)
- Green down + Blue down (both down) = illiquid, dealers absorb longs
- Green up + Blue up (both up) = illiquid, dealers absorb shorts
- **Only illiquid states correspond with major market moves**

**RULE 2 (dealer position):**
- Dealers net long (red > 0) → market will not crash during this state
- This is historical observation, not theoretical — 100% record across the report's history
- When dealers flip to net long, quarter-scale reversal is imminent (timing: within the quarter)
- Rationale: "He who holds the inventory sets the price" — dealers won't crash prices on their own inventory

### On "banks hold the bag" reasoning
- Beanie Babies / De Beers / ticket scalping analogy
- Whoever holds the supply controls the price
- Dealers absorbing market selling → they become the supply holders
- They won't crash the market they've just bought
- This is the mechanical reason for Rule 2, not mysticism

### On COT timing limitations
- Weekly publication with one-week lag → effectively two weeks stale
- NOT usable for day trading directly
- Useful for quarter-scale positioning: "the reversal is this quarter"
- Needs a daily signal for precision → enter Monthly Maps

### On Monthly Maps / COT correspondence
- Monthly Maps is effectively a daily-updated, 3-months-forward COT
- Bull zone bottom slope on Monthly Maps ↔ green line slope on COT
- Bear zone top slope on Monthly Maps ↔ blue line slope on COT
- Both are measuring the same institutional positioning phenomenon
- Monthly Maps uses OPRA (options, daily) vs. COT (futures, weekly)

### On the combined workflow (quarter + day)
1. Check COT once per week (Fridays)
2. Identify when dealers flip net long → quarter-scale reversal window opens
3. Watch Monthly Maps daily for the big green day that breaks from bear zone into bull zone
4. Load calls on that day
5. This is the instructor's primary options-buying trigger for major reversal plays

### On data blackouts and corruption awareness
- Government data outages (CFTC shutdowns) can coincide with large institutional repositioning
- When data resumes, check if reported changes match observable price action
- If slope changes don't reconcile with price flow, something is being hidden
- COVID 2020 = primary historical example
- December 2025 (recording date) = another active blackout — be cautious on resumption

### On commodities vs. indices
- Commodities (gold, silver, copper, oil) have directional inversions
- Gold: leveraged funds NET LONG (inflation hedge); asset managers NET SHORT
- Indices: inverse of that
- Same fan/pinch rules apply
- Same dealer net-long = floor rule applies universally

## Tactical Takeaways (Actionable)

1. **Memorize the A+ setups list** — these are the "EST" trades to take no matter what:
   - MHP bounce
   - WHP bounce (A, not A+, but close)
   - Bear Zone Top (Liquidity Pocket)
   - Bull Zone Bottom
   - DD Band Top/Bottom
2. **Check COT every Friday** (or on weekly release) — note dealer net position
3. **If dealers flip net long after a selloff:** start watching Monthly Maps daily for the bull-reclaim candle
4. **Load long calls on the bull-zone-reclaim candle** when both signals align
5. **Read COT by slope pairs, not single lines:** fan/pinch is liquid; both-same-direction is illiquid
6. **For commodities, use DAG report** — same analysis, different categories
7. **During government shutdowns, treat newly-published COT data skeptically** — verify slope changes match price action
8. **Size confluence-weighted:** B- = small; A+ = normal/large. Take the trade regardless (for longs).

## Connections to FuturesEdgeAI

Lesson 6 turns the abstract Greater Market signal into directly pullable government data and gives a clean setup taxonomy for the project.

### Direct implementation opportunities

- **COT data ingestion:**
  - CFTC publishes TFF report weekly (Fridays, 3:30pm ET)
  - Free via CFTC website (historical + current)
  - Data formats: CSV/JSON downloadable
  - Straightforward weekly cron job to pull asset managers, leveraged funds, dealers, other reportables for ES, NQ, GC (MES/MNQ/MGC)
  - Stored as time-series of net positions per category

- **COT state classifier:**
  - For each weekly snapshot, compute slopes of green and blue lines over N weeks (N = 4-8?)
  - Classify into: fanning apart / pinching together / both down / both up
  - Flag dealer state: net long (> 0) or net short (< 0)
  - Expose as a weekly signal that feeds into overall Greater Market score

- **Dealer net-long floor alert:**
  - When dealers transition from net short to net long: high-conviction "reversal watch" alert
  - Pair with Monthly Maps watch for bull-zone reclaim candle
  - Trigger: combined alert when both conditions met in same week
  - This is the explicit options-buying trigger the instructor uses

- **Setup handout as system constant:**
  - Encode the A+/A/B+/B- hold rates as reference data
  - Attach rating to every alert the system generates
  - Example: "MHP bounce, confluence: full (MHP res positive, Greater Market all bullish) — rated A+ (90%)"
  - User can configure minimum rating to display

- **Monthly Maps / COT correspondence verification:**
  - The project can validate its own Monthly Maps implementation by comparing slopes against actual COT data
  - If bull-zone-bottom slope ↔ green line slope, the options-derived Monthly Maps computation is internally consistent
  - Useful regression-test material: "when COT shows dealer flip, did Monthly Maps show bull-zone reclaim in the same window?"

- **Data blackout detection:**
  - Monitor CFTC publication dates; if missed, flag data gap
  - On data resumption, compute delta vs. prior published values
  - If slope changes exceed a threshold relative to observable futures price movement, raise anomaly alert
  - Useful for a "data integrity" pane in the dashboard
