# Lesson 19: Trader Funding Program Masterclass — Mastery Trading Each Account (FINAL LESSON)

## Quick Reference

**Core thesis:** The final lesson. Part 2 of TFP Masterclass. Each drawdown type requires different execution. The ultimate goal: use props to bankroll your own live account, then exit the prop game entirely.

**Static Drawdown Rules:**
- First trade = best trade
- A-plus long setups only (Day 1)
- VIX < BBB only (Day 1)
- Buy on 80-90% off sales, stockpile
- ETF > Apex (better buffer)
- After buffer: trade freely

**Trailing Drawdown Rules:**
- Base hits only (40pt NQ / 10pt ES)
- Never home runs
- Runner < half position
- Every trade A-plus until buffer
- Converts to static at initial buffer hit
- VIX > BBB without buffer → don't touch

**End-of-Day (EOD) Rules:**
- Normal trading style
- DLL/2 per trade if DLL exists
- Time-in-market is main threat
- Matt's preferred type (FundedNext Legacy, MyFundedFutures)

**The Apex Squeeze:**
- Firms give too-large contract allowances
- 2 E-minis on $625 DD = guaranteed blowup
- Correct: 2-4 MICROS, not minis
- "Knife to hold on slippery surface"

**NQ vs ES for tight accounts:** **ES wins.** NQ has 3-5 tick bid-ask spread = $15-25/side drag.

**The Consistency Hack (Matt's $200K Tradeify technique):**
- Blow consistency ON PURPOSE on first cycle
- Build massive $25-50K buffer
- Subsequent payout cycles zero-risk of blowup
- Trade $900/day instead of $5000/day once past first cycle

**THE ULTIMATE CAPSTONE:**
> "Nobody makes a living out of prop trading. Zero people. Your ultimate goal to trade props is to not need them."

**Self-funding pipeline:**
```
Props → SIM-funded payouts → More evals (self-funding prop machine)
     → Excess → Live account (better taxes, no splits)
     → Excess → Own account (no fees, no splits, 60/40 taxes)
     → Own account funds lifestyle
```

**Affiliate honesty quote:**
> "My incentive financially is to tell you love the prop system. I'm telling you get out as fast as possible."

## Statistical Claims (Testable)

### Position sizing by account type
- Apex $625 DD, 3 days, 2 trades/day → $208/trade → 2 micros NQ / 6 micros ES

- Matt's typical max on stream: 2 NQ or 4 ES

### Bid-ask spread drag
- NQ spread: 3-4 ticks normal, 5+ in volatility
- NQ cost per side: $15-25 market order
- NQ round trip: $30-50+ on desperation
- ES spread: ~0-1 tick, negligible

### NQ/ES scaling ratio
- 1 NQ strike (40pt) = $800
- 1 ES strike (10pt) = $500
- Ratio: 5/8 for equivalent risk
- 1 ES mini ≈ 6 NQ micros

### Martingale theoretical
- Pass probability: requires infinite iterations + infinite money
- Carnot cycle = theoretical perfect engine
- Real world: capped by capital
- Sale-enabled: near-zero cost per attempt

### Apex static vs ETF static
- Apex: $625 DD on $2000 PT = 2.5:1 ratio = 4+ trades to target
- ETF: $1000 DD on $2000 PT = 2:1 ratio = 2 trades to target
- ETF: $2000 DD on $2000 PT = 1:1 ratio = 1 trade target possible
- ETF cost only ~$100 more for 2× buffer

## Principles & Rules

### On why TFPs squeeze you
- They don't have traditional casino edge
- Edge = giving unrealistic rules you wouldn't self-impose
- Consistency + DLL combine to force "perfect P&L shape"
- Most traders can't shape P&L that precisely
- Time-in-market works against you: more days = more blowup chances

### On static account execution
- First trade must be best trade
- A-plus setup (bull zone bottom, bear zone top, MHP, DD band)
- Long side only (70% green days advantage)
- VIX < BBB (overpriced volatility = strong liquidity)
- Two trades per day maximum
- Position size: DD / 3 days / 2 trades / stop_points × $/pt
- Apex max contract offerings are traps — use micros

### On trailing drawdown execution
- Every trade must be A-plus (no exceptions until buffer)
- Base hits: 40pt NQ, 10pt ES — one strike max
- Close most of position at target
- Runner < 1/2 original position
- Never hold for home runs
- Close in green, not red
- Avoid VIX > BBB entirely on trailing
- Once buffer earned, account becomes fresh static Day 1

### On EOD drawdown execution
- Normal trading style OK
- No specific first-trade restriction
- Time-in-market is main threat
- Consistency rule usually limits daily max
- DLL (if present) → per-trade loss = DLL/2
- Two trades per day rule still applies
- Best account type for most traders (Matt's preference)

### On the Apex squeeze (universal firm tactic)
- Firms give too-large contract sizes
- Example: 2 E-minis on $625 DD (blowup on single 10pt move)
- Trap: desperation trading ("Hail Mary back to top")
- Solution: IGNORE firm's max, use budgeting formula
- The bigger the allowance, the dumber they think you are

### On NQ vs ES for tight accounts
- NQ bid-ask spread: 3-5 ticks = $15-25/side
- ES bid-ask spread: negligible
- Market orders cost you $30-50 round trip on NQ
- On $625 DD, that's 5-8% of budget in spread alone
- **Rule: tight accounts should trade ES, not NQ**

### On burn-and-churn strategy
- Only viable when accounts are 80-90% off
- Same budget buys 10× more accounts
- Even at 10% pass rate, total passes scale with volume
- Math: cost per pass approaches zero
- Take A-plus long setups only
- Still follow framework — not random YOLOs

### On pseudo-Martingale
- True Martingale requires infinite capital
- Carnot cycle analogy (mathematical limit)
- Near-zero account cost approximates infinite capital
- Enables strategies impossible at full price
- Not gambling — mathematical optimization on cheap inputs

### On firm rule bypass principles
- "Any rule firms ban is because it works"
- No DCA → close flat, re-enter with size
- Consistency → blow on purpose to build buffer
- DLL → wait for next day, but can't Martingale
- No HFT → closed (bots profited historically)
- Rules accumulated from every exploit firms discovered

### On no-DCA workaround
- DCA = dollar cost average (add to existing position)
- Close entire position first → zero
- Re-open at new level → single new trade
- Not DCA by definition
- No rule against multiple daily trades
- If firm flags you, they're wrong — social media pressure

### On consistency rule hack (Matt's signature)
- Standard: follow consistency, payout in 5 days, back to blowup line
- Hack: blow consistency intentionally, build $25-50K buffer
- First payout delayed (month+ vs 5 days)
- But: payouts 2-6 are risk-free
- Tradeify $200K buffer anecdote via this technique

### On post-payout fresh Day 1
- Payout removes gains, leaves small buffer
- Next cycle = fresh account risk
- Treat Day 1 post-payout as new static Day 1
- Wait for A-plus setup
- Don't trade immediately
- Same rule applies to post-eval funded Day 1

### On no-DLL preference (Matt's personal)
- DLL limits daily profit: DLL/2 per trade on 2:1 R:R = DLL × R:R as max daily
- No-DLL: max daily profit = total_loss × R:R
- Enables bigger scaling on good days
- Why FundedNext Legacy + MyFundedFutures preferred

### On request your payout timing
- Rule: never request > 50% of current balance
- Example: made $20K → request $10K
- Matt's rule: "Make $200K, request $100K"
- Buffer preservation prevents near-zero cycling

### On live account reality
- Tiny size: $5-20K typical
- Tight DLL: $1000 typical
- Small contracts: 2 max typical
- BUT: no profit split with time, no fees, 60/40 tax
- Better retention than SIM-funded

### On live account negotiation
- First live account = negotiable
- Firms are investing in you
- Can ask for: no DLL, bigger contracts, better splits
- Matt removed DLL on MyFundedFutures live
- Worst case: "no"
- Best case: better terms most traders never see

### On the data feed arbitrage (historical)
- Paper feeds had micro-delay vs real
- Bots detected real movement, bought paper bid
- Sold at real ask 1-2 ticks later
- Thousands per trade, thousands of trades
- Firms closed via no-HFT rule
- Lesson: every rule is a reaction to an exploit

### On "nobody makes a living from props"
- Taxes: 37% on SIM-funded (1099)
- Profit splits: 10-50% to firm
- Combined: 40-60% of gross lost
- Own account: 80% net retention
- Props are bankroll, not lifestyle
- Instructor admission: "I make much less from props than my real accounts. By a fraction. Laughably far away."

### On the ultimate goal
- Step 1: Prop machine self-funds
- Step 2: Excess → live account
- Step 3: Excess from live → own account
- Step 4: Own account funds lifestyle
- End state: don't need props anymore

### On the 5% bankroll rule
- Never put > 5% of capital in one account
- 20 chances to blow up
- Desperate-you will use everything available
- Keep 95% in savings/checking
- Hands-off safety net

### On the double-and-halve rule
- Account doubles → withdraw half
- Balance never decreases (except on deposits)
- Savings grows only
- Compound growth via repetition
- Matt's friend doubled money over years this way

### On affiliate honesty (instructor's admission)
- Financial incentive: keep you in props (commissions)
- Honest advice: exit props as fast as possible
- Priority: trader welfare > affiliate revenue
- Unusual transparency for a prop affiliate

### On the bootcamp's true arc
- L1-L16: Trading framework
- L17: Synthesis (three tenets, three failure modes)
- L18: TFP basics and budgeting
- L19: TFP mastery + EXIT strategy
- Props are practice, not profession
