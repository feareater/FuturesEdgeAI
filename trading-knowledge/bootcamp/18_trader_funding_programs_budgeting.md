# Lesson 18: Trader Funding Program Masterclass — Choosing a Prop Firm and Budgeting

## Quick Reference

**Core thesis:** TFPs are the ideal educational middle ground. Set tuition budget with spouse BEFORE signing up. Budget determines everything downstream.

**The SIM-funded loophole:**
- Your paper trading = "market research"
- Firm pays 1099 wages for the research
- Without loophole = illegal "bucket shop"
- Regulators may close eventually; reverts to traditional prop if so

**The Three Rules Triumvirate (every firm has 1-3):**
1. **Consistency** — max % of total in single day
2. **Daily Loss Limit (DLL)** — soft breach, stops day
3. **Payout Cap** — max withdrawal per period

No firm has all three gone (would be total firm risk).

**The Three Drawdown Types:**
1. **Static** — fixed at $0; easiest; most expensive ($$$)
2. **End-of-Day (EOD)** — static intraday, trails at close; middle
3. **Trailing** — trails $3K off running high; hardest; cheapest ($)

**The Budgeting Formula:**
```
Budget → Accounts/month → Days/account → Daily loss → Trades/day → Loss/trade → Position size
$2000/mo → 8 accts  → 3 days    → $1000     → 2         → $500       → 1 ES mini
```

**The 99% fail math (coin flip):**
- Pass prob = 1/(2^n); day 7 = <1%
- Shorter TIM = higher survival

**Vetted firms (2025):**
FundedNext, MyFundedFutures, ETF, Tradeify, Apex, TakeProfit
(Fast Track went bankrupt — cautionary tale)

**Most important quote:**
> "If you can't manage money, you can't trade. Because you know what trading is? Managing money. Trading is the art form of managing money under stress in a random environment you can't control."

## Key Concepts (Defined Terms)

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Trader Funding Program (TFP)** | Modern SIM-funded prop firm. Pay fee → prove yourself on SIM → receive real-money payouts for SIM gains. Proper name (instructor prefers over "prop firm"). |
| **SIM funded** | The middle step unique to 2020s TFPs. SIM account with real 1099 wage payouts. Not traditional prop, not pure paper. |
| **Market research loophole** | Legal mechanism for SIM-funded payouts. Your trades = research data; firm pays for data; 1099 contractor. |
| **Bucket shop** | Illegal: taking bets without exchange-backed execution. What firms would be WITHOUT the loophole. |
| **Traditional prop firm** | Wall Street standard: prove yourself → real capital. Legal. Has been around decades. |
| **Eval (evaluation)** | Initial SIM phase. Pay fee, pass target, advance to funded. |
| **Sim-funded** | Post-eval phase. Still SIM but earning real 1099 wages. |
| **Live** | Traditional prop style: real capital, real futures, 1256 tax treatment. |
| **Straight-to-funded (S2F)** | Skip the eval phase. Pay extra, start at funded immediately. Removes psychological barrier. Tradeify pioneered. |
| **Direct-to-funded** | Synonym for straight-to-funded. ETF uses this term. |
| **Funded** | Post-eval status. Payouts available per plan. |
| **Maximum Loss Limit (MLL)** | Maximum drawdown before account ends. THE real account size. Marketing size doesn't matter. |
| **Drawdown** | How much you can lose before account blows up. |
| **Static drawdown** | Fixed at starting balance. Doesn't move. Easiest to pass. |
| **End-of-Day (EOD) drawdown** | Static intraday; stair-steps up at close. Hybrid. |
| **Trailing drawdown** | Moves with your running high. Unrealized gains can blow you up. Hardest. |
| **Initial buffer** | Typically the drawdown amount. On trailing, once you hit +$3K, drawdown converts to static. |
| **Consistency rule** | Limit on % of total target achievable in single day. Forces stair-stepping. |
| **Daily Loss Limit (DLL)** | Max daily loss before soft breach. Account continues next day. |
| **Soft breach** | Broke DLL; done for day; account survives. |
| **Hard breach** | Hit MLL; account over. |
| **Payout cap** | Max withdrawal per period (e.g., $3K/5 days). Forces continued trading. |
| **Time in Market (TIM)** | Days from start to first payout. Lower = higher pass rate. |
| **Profit target** | Amount you must gain to pass eval or earn payout. |
| **Profit split** | Trader's share of profits (e.g., 80/20, 90/10, 100/0). |
| **100/0 split** | FundedNext. Trader keeps everything. Too good to be true? |
| **Activation fee** | Additional fee once you pass eval. Apex, ETF have these. |
| **Monthly fee** | Recurring fee. Mostly gone now. Topstep still has. |
| **All-in cost** | Total = eval fee + activation fee + monthly fees + data. Real cost. |
| **Cost per day** | Fee ÷ days to first payout. Lower = better. |
| **Drawdown-to-cost ratio** | MLL ÷ all-in cost. >9 great, >10 off charts, >12 extreme value. |
| **Coin-flip survival math** | Pass probability = 1/(2^n). Day 7 <1%. |
| **Three rules triumvirate** | Consistency, DLL, Payout Cap. Every firm has 1-3. None has 0. |
| **Three drawdown types** | Static, EOD, Trailing. |
| **Tuition budget** | Psychological frame: trading budget = college tuition. Gone by year-end. Maybe nothing but education in return. |
| **Partner aggro** | Family dissatisfaction when trading losses exceed verbal agreement. Avoid via upfront budget negotiation. |
| **Fast Track bankruptcy** | Cautionary tale: firm gave too-generous rules, ran out of money, went under. Lesson: too good to be true = scam or bankruptcy. |
| **Subjective payout denial** | Pattern seen in sketchy firms. Avoid firms that do this. |
| **Scale plan / Core plan / Pro plan** | MyFundedFutures tiers (cheap → expensive). |
| **Legacy plan** | FundedNext's original/slow plan. 7 days, uncapped. |
| **Rapid plan** | FundedNext's fast plan. 4 days, smaller caps. |
| **Lightning Funded** | ETF plan with DLL. |
| **Swing trading plan** | ETF time-limited trailing variant. |
| **Self-imposed DLL** | Your budget's daily limit (e.g., $1000/day) regardless of firm's DLL rule. |
| **The 3-day rule** | Matt's rule of thumb: plan to blow up each account in 3 days. Sets daily loss limit. |
| **The 2-trade rule** | Matt's aggression level: 2 trades/day max. Others may choose 3-5. |

## Principles & Rules

### On SIM vs Real vs TFP
- SIM-only training = waste of time (no emotion)
- Real environment = best education but most costly
- TFP = cost-effective education with real emotion
- SIM 1-2 days OK for platform familiarity only

### On emotion as education
- Learning happens at the "oh shit I'm about to blow up" moment
- Paper accounts have zero such moments
- Money on the table creates emotional stakes
- Stakes create learning

### On the loophole
- SIM gains classified as "market research"
- Firm pays for research data as 1099 wages
- Regulators may close loophole eventually
- If closed: reverts to traditional prop (SIM → live)
- Everything in this class still applies

### On tax implications
- TFP payouts = 1099 wages
- Up to 37% tax at top bracket
- Real futures = 60/40 rule ≈ 20% effective
- Higher income = bigger relative benefit from real account

### On the coin flip math
- Assume YOLO daily (worst case)
- Pass prob halves each day
- Day 7-8 = survival zone where most fail
- TIM is the dominant variable
- Minimize TIM = maximize survival

### On the Three Rules Triumvirate
- Every firm has 1-3 active
- No firm has 0 (would be all firm risk)
- Easier rules = higher price (firm carrying risk)
- Rock-paper-scissors: you give one, you take one
- Match rules to your trading style

### On consistency rule mechanics
- Max/day = consistency % × profit target
- Break it → target increases
- Forces stair-stepping P&L
- Adds days to TIM
- Hurts trader if time-pressured

### On DLL mechanics
- Soft breach: done for day, account alive
- Limits martingaling into reversals
- Limits EST re-attack strategy
- Matt hates it (blocks his style)
- Struggling traders may WANT it (blocks spiraling)

### On payout cap mechanics
- Limits withdrawal per period
- Forces continued trading
- Invites blowup
- Risk model: firm bets you'll blow up before taking full balance

### On the three drawdown types
- Static: easiest, most expensive
- EOD: middle
- Trailing: hardest, cheapest
- Trailing converts to static at initial buffer
- Matt's preference: EOD (moderate cost, mostly static)

### On trailing drawdown trap
- News event pumps account +$10K
- Pullback: still profitable but breached trail
- You "made money wrong way" and blow up
- Why trailing is hardest
- Why trailing firms run 90% off sales (high blowup rate)

### On drawdown-to-cost ratio
- MLL ÷ all-in cost = true value
- >9 great, >10 off charts, >12 extreme
- FundedNext Legacy 50K: 15.625 (best in EOD class)
- MyFundedFutures: ~10 range
- Use this to compare across firms

### On MLL = real account size
- Marketing says "100K account"
- MLL $3K = actual $3K account
- Zeros above MLL don't matter
- Always compare by MLL

### On all-in cost calculation
- Eval fee + activation fee + monthly × duration + data
- Apex: $30 eval (sale) + $220 activation = $250 all-in
- FundedNext: flat $80-250 (no activation) = same all-in
- Watch for hidden fees

### On time in market as the critical variable
- Lower TIM = higher pass probability
- Shorter TIM usually = higher price
- FundedNext Rapid: 4 days (fastest) + cheapest = too good to be true?
- Current industry floor: 4 days
- Instructor's advice: prioritize TIM for most traders

### On firm selection by trader profile
- Consistent/profitable: no-DLL, moderate consistency
- Struggling: static, with DLL (protective)
- Aggressive: no-consistency once funded
- Budget-constrained: trailing with 90% sales

### On "every single time" vs DLL
- Matt trades every EST setup every time
- DLL cuts off re-attacks after losses
- Matt therefore avoids DLL firms
- Personal preference based on strategy

### On Fast Track bankruptcy lesson
- Too-generous rules → firm risk → bankruptcy
- Cautionary for any firm with all 3 rules lax + cheap + quick TIM
- Always verify payouts with community
- Watch for subjective denial patterns

### On the tuition budget philosophy
- Set budget with partner BEFORE signing up
- Treat as 2-year tuition commitment
- Assume entire budget is GONE by year-end
- Reward: probably nothing but education
- 3-5 year horizon normal for profitability

### On partner alignment
- "Trading is easiest way to lose everyone you love"
- Upfront budget negotiation essential
- Family buys in → no aggro when losing
- Public framing: "student" not "gambler"

### On the budgeting formula
- Budget → accounts → days → daily loss → trade count → loss/trade → size
- Mechanical, formulaic
- No intuition required
- Output: exact position size

### On the 3-day rule
- Plan each account to last 3 days
- Sets daily loss = MLL/3
- Creates business plan
- Ensures budget predictability

### On the 2-trade rule (Matt's version)
- 2 trades/day max
- Because 80-90% ideal hold rate
- Rare double-loss in single day
- Personal aggression level

### On self-imposed DLL
- Budget's daily limit overrides firm's DLL (when budget is tighter)
- $1000/day on $2000/month budget
- Stops before firm even stops you

### On Matt's preference rationale
- EOD drawdown preferred (stair-step, moderate cost)
- No-DLL firms preferred (strategy-friendly)
- No activation fee preferred (simpler math)
- FundedNext satisfies all three

### On sale-hopping strategy
- Don't marry one firm
- Bounce where sales are
- Learn all drawdown types
- Community streams announce sales
- Watch for 90% off trailings

### On the household alignment conversation
- "I'm committing 2 years"
- "Budgeted to not affect cost of living"
- "I'll still take you on dates, see parents"
- "Probably nothing but education at end"
- Get explicit sign-off

### On the business plan emergence
- Start: "want to trade prop firms"
- Result: detailed position sizing with family alignment
- Natural outcome of proper budgeting
- Most traders never do this → most fail

### On the smiley face math
- Budget respected → family happy
- Predictable loss → no panic
- Emotional stability → better trading
- Everyone wins

## Tactical Takeaways (Actionable)

1. **Have the budget conversation BEFORE signing up for any TFP** — with spouse/partner/family.
2. **Frame as 2-year tuition commitment** — "probably nothing but education at the end."
3. **Use the budgeting formula:** budget → accounts → days → daily loss → trades → position size.
4. **Match firm rules to your trading style** — consistent traders: no-DLL; struggling: static; aggressive: no-consistency once funded.
5. **Prioritize Time in Market (TIM)** — shorter TIM = higher pass rate.
6. **Choose drawdown type by budget and skill:** trailing (cheap practice), EOD (balance), static (confidence boost).
7. **Calculate drawdown-to-cost ratio** — target >9, ideal >10.
8. **Compare all-in cost** — eval + activation + monthly + data.
9. **Watch for 90% off sales** — especially on trailing firms.
10. **Don't marry one firm** — bounce between sales.
11. **Remember MLL is real account size** — ignore marketing.
12. **Track your self-imposed DLL** — budget's limit, not firm's.
13. **Matt's 3-day rule: plan each account to last 3 days.**
14. **Matt's 2-trade rule: 2 trades/day max.**
15. **Avoid firms with all 3 triumvirate rules lax** — likely going bankrupt or scamming.
16. **Stick to vetted firms:** FundedNext, MyFunded, ETF, Tradeify, Apex, TakeProfit.
17. **Use Discord spreadsheet** for position sizing calculations.
