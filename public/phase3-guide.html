Phase 3 + Bias Dashboard — Trading Guide

A complete walkthrough for reading the FuturesEdge Phase 3 indicator alongside the Bias Dashboard, making trade decisions, and using the AI webhook to get Claude commentary on every setup.

Phase 3 — What it does

All chart overlays (EMA, VWAP, IOF zones, trendlines) plus setup detection and structured JSON alerts that fire to your FuturesEdge server.

Bias Dashboard — What it does

A standalone multi-timeframe bias panel. Reads your chart TF and the two higher timeframes automatically. Shows EMA stack, VWAP, RSI, and volume per TF.

Why use both together

Phase 3 fires at the bar level — it sees the setup on your TF. The Bias Dashboard tells you whether the higher timeframes agree. Agreement = higher probability.

The AI loop

Every Phase 3 alert fires a JSON webhook to FuturesEdge. The server calls Claude with full market context and returns a plain-language assessment within seconds.

Which scripts to run: Add FuturesEdge AI — Phase 3 AI and FuturesEdge — Bias Dashboard to the same chart. You do not need Phase 1 or Phase 2 — Phase 3 includes everything from both. The Bias Dashboard is a separate indicator added on top.

Chart Setup

Run both indicators on your primary trading chart. Phase 3 works on any timeframe but is designed for 5m, 15m, and 30m. The Bias Dashboard auto-detects your chart TF and shows the next two higher timeframes.

Reading the Bias Dashboard

The Bias Dashboard is your first check before you look at any setup. It tells you whether the trend on your timeframe is backed by institutional momentum on the higher timeframes. A Phase 3 setup that agrees with all three columns is significantly higher probability than one that fights the higher TF.

The panel has four columns: a row label, your current TF, HTF1 (one step up), and HTF2 (two steps up). Each column is independently scored.

The Five Bias States

Each column shows one of five states. Read the top row first — it's the headline bias for that timeframe:

StateMeaningAction
▲ BULLISH EMA full stack aligned (9 > 21 > 50) and price above VWAP Actively look for long setups
▼ BEARISH EMA full stack aligned (9 < 21 < 50) and price below VWAP Actively look for short setups
▲ Lean Bull EMA stack bullish but price below VWAP, or VWAP bullish but EMAs mixed Longs only, reduce size — bias incomplete
▼ Lean Bear EMA stack bearish but price above VWAP, or vice versa Shorts only, reduce size
◆ Above / Below VWAP EMAs not stacked (no clear trend), only VWAP position gives any signal Avoid directional trades; range plays only

Confidence percentage

Below the bias state each column shows a directional confidence percentage (0–100%). This is how strongly the indicators are agreeing — not a prediction, a measurement of current alignment strength.

ConfidenceMeaningPosition sizing
80% +Strong conviction — all factors alignedFull size
60 – 79%Moderate conviction — most factors alignedNormal size
Below 60%Weak or conflicted — at least one factor disagreesHalf size or skip

Timeframe Alignment — The Golden Rule

The highest-probability trades occur when all three timeframe columns point the same direction. This means institutional order flow is aligned across multiple timeframes — the trend has depth, not just momentum.

Green light example (5m chart):

5m: ▲ BULLISH 84%  ·  15m: ▲ BULLISH 77%  ·  30m: ▲ LEAN BULL 68%

All three pointing up with strong scores. Take bullish Phase 3 setups at full size.

Yellow light example:

5m: ▲ BULLISH 79%  ·  15m: ▲ LEAN BULL 61%  ·  30m: ◆ Below VWAP 52%

30m is neutral or opposing. Take longs at half size only. Tighten the stop.

Red light example:

5m: ▲ BULLISH 81%  ·  15m: ▼ BEARISH 74%  ·  30m: ▼ BEARISH 68%

5m is fighting both higher timeframes. A bullish Phase 3 signal here is a counter-trend trap. Hard pass regardless of the confidence score on the Phase 3 label.

Rule of thumb: Require at least 2 of 3 columns to agree with your setup direction. All 3 aligned = full conviction. 2 of 3 = proceed with caution. Only 1 or a split = skip or wait for resolution.

RSI and Volume Rows

RSI(14) — the exhaustion warning

The dashboard shows RSI for each timeframe column. Normal values (30–70) just confirm direction. Watch for the warning flags:

DisplayWhat it meansAction
⚠ OB 74 RSI overbought (>70). Even in a bullish regime, price is stretched. Buying here is buying exhaustion. Reduce long size or wait for pullback to zone
⚠ OS 27 RSI oversold (<30). A short setup here is selling into a bounce zone. Reduce short size or wait for bounce to confirm continuation
62 (no flag) Normal momentum. Mildly bullish (>50). No exhaustion risk. No adjustment needed

Volume row (current TF only)

Volume compares the last closed candle against its 20-bar average. This tells you whether the move behind a Phase 3 signal has institutional backing.

DisplayMeaningAction
▲ HIGH ×2.1Bullish candle with 2.1× average volumeConfirms conviction — execute
▲ norm ×1.1Normal volume on up candleProceed normally
▲ low ×0.6Bullish candle with weak volumeNo institutional follow-through — reduce size or wait

Phase 3 — The EMA Stack

The three EMAs (9, 21, 50) are the primary trend regime filter. Their relative alignment tells you which direction the current timeframe is trending. Phase 3 uses a subtle background tint and fill between EMA 9 and 21 to make the regime visible at a glance.

Bullish regime

EMA 9 > EMA 21 > EMA 50 with green fill between 9 and 21, and a faint green background tint. Only take longs in this state.

Bearish regime

EMA 9 < EMA 21 < EMA 50 with red fill, faint red tint. Only take shorts in this state.

Mixed / crossed EMAs (e.g. 9 > 21 but 21 < 50) indicate a range or transitioning market. Zone rejections can still work in range conditions, but PDH breakouts and trendline breaks have lower quality in this state.

VWAP and Bands

The VWAP (cyan line) is the volume-weighted average price for the current session. It resets at the RTH open (09:30 ET). Price above VWAP = buyers are in control; below VWAP = sellers. The ±1σ and ±2σ bands show where price is statistically stretched from value.

LevelSignificance
VWAP lineSession midpoint — acts as dynamic support in uptrends, resistance in downtrends
±1σ bandsFirst extension — price rejects here in trending sessions; dashed circles on chart
±2σ bandsSecond extension (crosses on chart) — price is stretched; mean-reversion risk

VWAP confluence bonus: Zone rejections within 0.3 × ATR14 of the VWAP line earn +8 confidence points automatically. When a zone rejection occurs right at VWAP, it's a supply/demand zone and a session value level rejecting simultaneously — meaningfully higher probability.

IOF Zones — The Colored Boxes

Institutional Order Flow (IOF) zones are the shaded boxes drawn on the chart. They represent price ranges where institutional activity was concentrated. When price returns to these zones, they often act as support or resistance.

Bullish FVG (green box)

A 3-candle fair value gap where candle 3's low is above candle 1's high. Institutional buying moved price faster than selling could fill — the gap is unfilled demand.

Bearish FVG (red box)

Inverse: candle 3's high is below candle 1's low. Unfilled supply. Price returning to this zone is returning to an institutional selling area.

Bullish OB (blue box)

The last bearish candle before a strong bullish impulse move (≥1.5× ATR). The candle must have a body ≤50% of its total range. Represents institutional demand that caused the impulse.

Bearish OB (dark red box)

Last bullish candle before a bearish impulse. Same criteria inverted. Institutional supply that caused the impulse downward.

Box lifecycle

Boxes always extend to the current bar — they resize with the chart at any zoom level. They disappear when the zone is used up:

EventWhat it means
Box is visibleZone is active — price has not fully entered it, or entered but not closed through it
FVG box disappearsPrice closed inside the FVG (filled) — demand/supply has been consumed
OB box disappearsPrice closed through the OB bottom (bullish) or top (bearish) — mitigated

An OB rejection (catching price at the OB rather than just any FVG) adds +5 confidence points to a zone rejection signal. The Phase 3 script differentiates between OB and FVG rejections in its scoring.

PDH / PDL Lines

The gray dashed horizontal lines show the Prior Day High (PDH) and Prior Day Low (PDL). These are the most-watched reference levels by institutional traders and are labeled at the start of each new day.

Price relative to PDH/PDLInterpretation
Clean close above PDH on volumeBullish momentum — breakout continuation likely. Look for PDH as new support on pullback.
Clean close below PDL on volumeBearish momentum — breakdown continuation likely.
Price choppy around PDH/PDLLevel is contested. No clean signal — wait for resolution.
Close back below PDH after breakoutFalse break — exit immediately if in a long.

Setup 1 — Zone Rejection

ZONE REJ ▲ / ▼

What it is: Price enters a supply or demand zone (OB or FVG), produces a significant wick (≥35% of the candle's total range), and closes back outside the zone in the rejection direction. The wick shows institutions defended the zone; the close back outside confirms it.

This is the primary signal type. Backtest result: 72.7% win rate when all scoring components align.

Entry

On the close of the rejection candle. Phase 3 fires on barstate.isconfirmed (bar close) — you enter at the open of the following bar.

Confirmation checklist

  • Bias Dashboard agrees with the signal direction (same color, ≥65%)
  • Phase 3 info table REGIME matches direction
  • Phase 3 info table 15m EMA matches direction
  • VWAP position matches (above VWAP for longs, below for shorts)
  • Volume is not low ×0.x on the rejection candle
  • RSI is not ⚠ OB against a long, or ⚠ OS against a short
  • Not within 09:30–10:00 ET or 15:45–16:00 ET (session open/close noise)

What kills the setup

  • EMA stack on the chart TF is counter-trend — Phase 3 penalizes this proportionally
  • Wick is marginal (below 35% of candle range) — the rejection is weak
  • Higher TF shows an open FVG or OB in the opposing direction — contested level
  • Price immediately wicks back into the zone on the next bar

Trade management

ParameterBullishBearish
Stop loss1× ATR below the zone low1× ATR above the zone high
Take profit (MNQ/MES)2:1 R:R (2× ATR from entry)
Take profit (MGC)1:1 R:R (1× ATR from entry)
Take profit (MCL)1.5:1 R:R (1.5× ATR from entry)
Exit signalClose back inside the zone = invalidation, exit immediately

Setup 2 — PDH / PDL Breakout

PDH BO ▲ / PDL BO ▼

What it is: The first bar to close above the Prior Day High (bullish) or below the Prior Day Low (bearish). This marks a momentum breakout through the most significant institutional reference level from the prior session.

Why it works with delayed data: Momentum from a PDH/PDL break typically lasts 15–45 minutes. Even with 15-minute delayed seed data on the FuturesEdge server, the signal is actionable — you're not trying to catch the exact bar, you're riding the continuation.

Entry

On the close of the breakout bar. Confirm the bar has actually closed above PDH — a wick above PDH that closes back below is not a breakout.

Confirmation checklist

  • Session is RTH (09:30–21:00 UTC) — the script gates this automatically
  • EMA regime aligned with direction (bullish for PDH, bearish for PDL)
  • 15m EMA also aligned with direction (+15 pts)
  • Price above VWAP at time of PDH break (+8 pts)
  • Volume expansion on the breakout candle (+10 pts)
  • Close is within 0.5× ATR of PDH (tight break, not extended)

What kills the setup

  • Counter-trend EMA stack — PDH break with bearish EMA stack is suspect
  • Close immediately back below PDH on the next bar — false break, exit
  • Near-open or near-close session noise (−10 pts automatically)
  • Breakout bar has low volume — no institutional backing

Trade management

ParameterValue
Stop loss1× ATR below PDH for longs (PDH becomes new support)
Hard stopClose back below PDH = exit — level failed
Take profit2:1 for MNQ/MES; next significant swing high or PDH ± 2× ATR
Pullback re-entryFirst retest of PDH as support after clean break — often better entry

Setup 3 — Trendline Break

TL BREAK ▲ / TL BREAK ▼

What it is: The first bar to close on the opposite side of the auto-drawn resistance trendline (bullish break — closes above) or support trendline (bearish break — closes below). The trendlines connect the two most recent confirmed swing points on your chart timeframe.

Trendline quality

Phase 3 draws trendlines from the two most recent swing highs (resistance) and swing lows (support). A trendline with more touches before the break is more significant. The script fires on any break of a 2-point trendline, but you should visually assess how many times price respected it:

Touches before breakQualitySizing
2 (minimum)Valid — but could be a coincidenceHalf size
3Good — price has respected the line multiple timesNormal size
4+High quality — well-established levelFull size

Confirmation checklist

  • Clean close through the trendline — not just a wick
  • Break distance > 0.3× ATR (Phase 3 adds +10 pts vs +5 for marginal breaks)
  • Volume expansion confirms the break (+10 pts)
  • Bias Dashboard agrees with direction
  • VWAP position matches (above VWAP for bullish TL break adds +10 pts)
  • EMA stack aligned with break direction

Trade management

ParameterValue
Stop lossJust back through the broken trendline (1× ATR or back inside)
Take profitNext significant level — PDH/PDL, prior swing high/low, ±2σ VWAP band
InvalidationClose back on the wrong side of the trendline = exit

Setup 4 — Break of Structure (BOS)

BOS ▲ / BOS ▼

What it is: A close above the most recent confirmed swing high (BOS ▲) or below the most recent confirmed swing low (BOS ▼). This signals a structural shift in the market — a new higher high or lower low has been made, confirming the current trend direction.

BOS is a context signal, not a standalone trade entry. By the time the BOS label fires (on bar close), the structural move has already happened — you've missed the entry. Use BOS to update your directional bias, then wait for the first pullback to a zone or VWAP for the actual entry.

How to use BOS correctly

  • BOS ▲ fires → update your bias to bullish for this TF → look for the next zone rejection off demand as your entry
  • BOS ▼ fires → update bias to bearish → look for zone rejection off supply
  • BOS also adds +15 confidence points to zone rejection signals detected in the following bars (handled by the FuturesEdge server)
  • Multiple BOS in the same direction without a pullback = overextended, wait

Decision Flowchart

Work through this sequence every time a Phase 3 label appears on the chart.

1
Check Bias Dashboard direction All 3 TFs agree → green light. 2 of 3 → yellow (half size). 1 of 3 or split → red (no trade).
2
Does signal direction match Bias Dashboard? If yes, continue. If no, hard pass — regardless of the confidence score.
3
Check Phase 3 info table REGIME, 15m EMA, and VWAP position should all match the signal direction. Note any that don't.
4
Check RSI in Bias Dashboard ⚠ OB against a long, or ⚠ OS against a short? Reduce size or wait for a pullback first.
5
Check volume row HIGH ×1.5+ → confirmed. norm → fine. low ×0.x → hesitate, watch next bar.
6
Execute at bar open (entry after signal bar closes) SL = 1× ATR from entry. TP = instrument R:R (MNQ/MES: 2:1, MGC: 1:1, MCL: 1.5:1).
7
Read AI commentary (bottom-right panel or FuturesEdge dashboard) If Claude says "wick marginal, volume below average" — consider sizing down even if score passed threshold.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Taking counter-trend signals. The Bias Dashboard showing ▼ BEARISH on the 30m while Phase 3 prints ZONE REJ ▲ is a warning, not an opportunity. The counter-trend EMA penalty reduces the score, but a signal can still pass 65%. The Bias Dashboard is your veto — use it.

Trading near RTH open (09:30–10:00 ET). The first 30 minutes are high volatility, wide spreads, and frequent false signals. The Phase 3 script applies −10 pts during this window automatically. Watch the first 30 minutes, don't trade them.

Chasing PDH/PDL breakouts. Enter on the close of the breakout bar — not 5 bars later when price has already moved 2× ATR. If you missed it, wait for the first pullback to PDH/PDL (now support/resistance) and look for a zone rejection there.

Ignoring the volume row. A ZONE REJ ▲ 84% on a ▲ low ×0.5 volume candle is a low-conviction rejection. The wick happened but nobody followed through. Wait for the next bar to confirm before entering.

Treating BOS as a trade entry. BOS fires at bar close — the structural move has already completed. Use it to update your bias and look for the next setup, not as a direct entry signal.

Webhook Setup

Phase 3 uses TradingView's alert() function to fire a structured JSON payload to your FuturesEdge server on every confirmed setup. This is what feeds the Claude AI commentary system.

Step 1 — Add the server endpoint

The webhook route POST /api/tv-webhook must be added to server/index.js. The full code is in the Setup Guide under "Phase 3 — AI Commentary Webhook."

Step 2 — Wire TradingView

  1. Press Alt+A on your chart to open the Create Alert dialog
  2. Set Condition to FuturesEdge AI — Phase 3 AI
  3. Set Trigger to Any alert() function call — this single alert catches all six setup types
  4. Under Notifications → enable Webhook URL
  5. Enter your server URL: http://<your-ip>:3000/api/tv-webhook (must be your LAN/public IP — not localhost)
  6. Leave the Message field blank — Phase 3 sends the full JSON payload automatically
  7. Set Expiry to open-ended → click Create

Important: Use Any alert() function call — NOT the named alertcondition() alerts like "FE · Bullish Zone Rejection." The named conditions send plain text. Only alert() sends the full JSON that the AI system needs.

Using AI Commentary

When a Phase 3 webhook fires, the FuturesEdge server calls Claude with the full market context. The response is a 3–5 sentence plain-language assessment that appears in the alert feed on the dashboard.

What the commentary tells you

  • What the setup is and why it triggered technically
  • Quality assessment — wick strength, volume, zone type
  • What would make the setup stronger (confirmation level)
  • What would invalidate it (where to place the stop / when to exit)

How to use it: Treat AI commentary as a second opinion, not a recommendation. If the score is 82% but Claude says "wick is marginal, volume below average, 15m is mixed" — that's telling you the score is being carried by the regime alignment, not the candle quality. Size down.

What it is NOT: The AI commentary is not a guarantee, not a signal to enter, and not a substitute for your own analysis. It surfaces context that can be missed when watching live charts. The final decision is always yours.
